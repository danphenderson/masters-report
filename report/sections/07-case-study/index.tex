\section{Idealized Stenosis Case Study}
\label{sec:case-study}
\label{sec:idealized-stenosis-case-study}

This chapter applies the review framework to one controlled stenosis example.
The purpose is not to treat the case as an independent validation study, but to
show how a reduced-model result should be read once the model state, boundary
approximation, discretization, observation rule, and metrics have been fixed.
Three findings matter most. First, the manufactured-solution test provides
positive implementation-verification evidence for the forced finite-volume
operator. Second, the geometry-rest test records that the selected
geometry-rest-balanced finite-volume realization preserves the declared
quiescent stenotic equilibrium to roundoff, while the older MUSCL/Rusanov
realization remains a nonselected limitation. Third, the final-time 1D--3D
comparison reports section-mean velocity discrepancies only after both states
are mapped to the same observables. The rest of the chapter makes those three
findings interpretable.

Figure~\ref{fig:stenosis-fem-fvm-meshes} orients the case study only: it
previews the stationary-Stokes finite-element sampling geometry, the section
cuts, and the one-dimensional finite-volume grid before the model
specification and result hierarchy are stated.
