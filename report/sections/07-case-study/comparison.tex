\subsubsection{Observation Operator and Deformed Coordinates}
\label{subsec:cross-sectional-sampling-protocol-results}

The comparison uses the specified cross-section quadrature operator. For a
target plane $S_j=\Omega_{\mathrm{3D}}\cap\{z=z_j\}$, the operator intersects
the tetrahedral mesh with the plane, triangulates the cut polygon, and computes
\begin{flalign*}
    \quad
    A_{3D,j}
    =
    \sum_{\Delta\in\mathcal Q_j}|\Delta|,
    \qquad
    Q_{3D,j}
    =
    \sum_{\Delta\in\mathcal Q_j}|\Delta|\,\bar u_{z,\Delta},
    \qquad
    \bar u_{3D,j}
    =
    Q_{3D,j}/A_{3D,j}. &&
\end{flalign*}
The 1D quantities use the solver-to-physical map
$Q_{1D,j}=\pi q(z_j,t)$ and $\bar u_{1D,j}=q(z_j,t)/a(z_j,t)$. In
deformed-coordinate mode the plane cut is formed after replacing each mesh
coordinate $x$ by $x+d(x)$, where $d$ is the node-centered resolved
displacement field.

The stenosis throat is the minimizer $z^\star=\argmin_z R_0(z)$ of the smooth
reference-radius profile in Eq.~\ref{eq:1d-stenosis-profile}. For the comparison
geometry this gives $z^\star\approx2.451\,\mathrm{cm}$, with
$R_{\min}=0.1386\,\mathrm{cm}$ for the 23\% stenosis case and
$R_{\min}=0.1080\,\mathrm{cm}$ for the 40\% stenosis case.
The area check below is a limitation on the velocity interpretation, not a
tolerance proof. Across the reported rows the maximum section-area discrepancy
is 4.60\% (4.46\% in the reference-coordinate 40\% case and 4.60\% in the
deformed-coordinate 40\% case). No tolerance or sensitivity result in this
report proves that this area mismatch is negligible. Because
$\bar u=Q_{\mathrm{phys}}/A_{\mathrm{phys}}$, area-denominator discrepancies can
directly affect mean-velocity interpretation, so the comparison remains a
section-observation diagnostic conditioned on the reported area check.

The algebraic split used for flow and area effects is
\begin{flalign*}
    \quad
    \frac{Q_{1D}}{A_{1D}}-\frac{Q_{3D}}{A_{3D}}

\subsubsection{Quasi-static Membrane-FSI Comparator}
\label{subsec:membrane-fsi-comparator}

The wall comparator solves a quasi-static fixed point between the
Stokes lumen solve and the radial membrane update. In the notation of
Appendix~\ref{app:num-membrane-fsi-validation}, the wall equation is the
inertia-free form
\begin{flalign*}
    \quad
    C_0\eta_r(z)=f_r(z),
    \qquad
    \eta_r(0)=\eta_r(L)=0,
    \qquad
    C_0=\frac{Eh}{(1-\sigma^2)(R_0^*)^2}. &&
\end{flalign*}
This is the Canic-style membrane relation used for the resolved comparator
\cite[Eq.~(35)]{CanicEtAl2024Extended1DStenosis}. It is separate from the
selected 1D pressure--area closure used in the reduced model. The
comparator is a quasi-static radial membrane update coupled to repeated Stokes
solves; it informs wall-motion scale under the specified comparator.

\subsubsection{Interpretation Limits}
\label{subsec:spatial-localization-discrepancies}

These comparisons should be read as diagnostic comparisons of observed
quantities, not as direct tests of model-tier equivalence. The reported
agreements and discrepancies are conditional on the chosen 1D model, its
closures, the finite-volume realization, the section-cut observation rule, and
the limited resolved data available at the matched final time. Because matched
transient wall histories, boundary histories, and a common pressure gauge are
not available for the primary two-case comparison, the results do not support
clinical validation, pressure-ratio claims, or reproduction of a fully matched
transient FSI computation. Appendix~\ref{app:code-and-ai-use} records the data
availability and supplementary Section~4.1 comparison details.
