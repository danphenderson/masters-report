\subsection{Diagnostic 1D--3D Velocity Comparison}
\label{sec:resolved-3d-comparison}

\subsubsection{Available 3D Data and Matching Limits}
\label{subsec:preliminary-cross-model-velocity-comparison}

This section reports the final-time resolved-velocity comparison whose protocol
is defined in Section~\ref{subsec:numerical-experiments-metrics-provenance}. It
is included because it illustrates the observation-operator discipline reviewed
in Sections~\ref{subsec:hemodynamic-observables} and
\ref{subsec:numerical-methods-observation-operators}: a reduced 1D state and a
resolved 3D velocity field can be compared only after both are mapped to common
velocity and physical-flow observables. The two available resolved velocity
fields are the 23\% and 40\% stenosis-severity cases, labelled C23 and C40.
The 1D runs and resolved samples are matched at $T=0.9995$ s, with recorded
final-time offsets of $5.47\times10^{-14}$ s for both cases.

That timestamp agreement establishes final-time alignment, not full dataset
matching. The zero-input rest-state test in
Section~\ref{sec:numerical-verification} remains
required context because it uses the same principal finite-volume operator as
the comparison runs and shows a nonzero geometry-rest defect on the production
grid. The current/deformed 3D geometry status also remains unresolved: the
static cut-area check below supports only the fixed-section geometry check, not a
decision about whether a deformed-wall geometry or displacement application
should be used for final-time plane cuts. The 3D wall model, wall-motion history,
inlet/outlet histories, material parameters, and prior transient history are not
carried by the comparison record; these would need to be established before
making a validation-level claim.

The comparison gates are therefore deliberately limited: the final timestamp is
matched, the static section area is checked, the dynamic or deformed 3D geometry
state is unresolved, the resolved flow is not sectionwise constant, and the
wall, boundary, material, and transient-history metadata needed for validation
are not persisted in the retained comparison record. No pressure-drop,
pressure-ratio, or FFR-style result is retained for this 1D--3D comparison
because compatible pressure observations and extraction conventions are not
declared for the paired data.

\subsubsection{Observation Operator}
\label{subsec:cross-sectional-sampling-protocol-results}

The comparison uses the declared
\texttt{CrossSectionQuadratureOperator}. For each target plane, the operator
intersects the tetrahedral 3D mesh with the plane, triangulates the cut polygon,
and computes section area, physical flow, and area-mean axial velocity from the
cut triangles as stated in Section~\ref{subsec:plane-tetrahedron-comparison-operator}.
The 1D quantities use the solver-to-physical map from
Definition~\ref{def:physical-to-solver-map}: $Q_{1D}=\pi q$ and
$\bar u_{1D}=q/a$. The principal comparison uses 200 equally spaced section
targets on $0\le z\le6$ cm. Secondary radial-profile outputs were generated at
$z=1.951$, 2.451, and 2.951 cm, but they are quarantined rather than used as
retained results.

The stenosis throat is the minimizer $z^\star=\argmin_z R_0(z)$ of the smooth
reference-radius profile in Eq.~\ref{eq:1d-stenosis-profile}. For the comparison
geometry this gives $z^\star\approx2.451\,\mathrm{cm}$ for both severities, with
$R_{\min}=0.1386\,\mathrm{cm}$ for C23 and $R_{\min}=0.1080\,\mathrm{cm}$ for
C40. The shaded region in Figure~\ref{fig:t1-section-mean-comparison} is the
displayed interval $2.4\le z\le2.8\,\mathrm{cm}$.

\subsubsection{Cut-Area Check}
\label{subsec:section-averaged-velocity-results}

The same quadrature record checks whether the cut operator is sampling the
expected static cross-section. With the reference area
$A_{\mathrm{ref}}(z_j)=\pi R_0(z_j)^2$, define
\begin{flalign*}
    \quad
    \epsilon_A(z_j)
    =
    \frac{|A_{3D}(z_j)-A_{\mathrm{ref}}(z_j)|}
         {A_{\mathrm{ref}}(z_j)}. &&
\end{flalign*}
Table~\ref{tab:t1-area-check} reports this geometry/operator check for the same
200 section targets. It shows sub-percent typical static cut-area discrepancy,
with the largest differences confined to a few targets. This check does not
resolve the current/deformed geometry question and does not measure the dynamic
area discrepancy entering the 1D velocity denominator,
\begin{flalign*}
    \quad
    \epsilon_A^{\mathrm{model}}(z_j,t)
    =
    \frac{|\pi a(z_j,t)-A_{3D}(z_j)|}{A_{3D}(z_j)}. &&
\end{flalign*}

\label{subsec:spatial-localization-discrepancies}

The result of this chapter is a localization of descriptive velocity
discrepancies under the declared observation operator. The section-mean
comparison localizes the largest C40 discrepancy upstream of the displayed
throat-centered band. This observation is bounded by the matching limits stated
at the start of the chapter: unmatched or unpersisted 3D wall, boundary,
material, history, and current/deformed geometry information; unresolved axial
variation in extracted 3D flow; and unmatched transient history before the
common final timestamp. The flow/area decomposition is bookkeeping, and the
displayed differences do not identify a unique mechanism.
