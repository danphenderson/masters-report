\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Abstract.}\par\smallskip
This report develops an auditable reduced-model workflow for comparing
stenotic-flow velocity outputs, rather than a broad physiological validation
study. The selected solver is the Julia \path{StenosisHemodynamics}
implementation of an $R_{\max}$-normalized Canic-derived one-dimensional
area--flow model, with physical area and flow mapped to the solver variables
$a=R^2$ and $q=Q_{\mathrm{phys}}/\pi$; the principal C23/C40 comparison uses
MUSCL finite volume with minmod-limited Rusanov fluxes and native SSPRK3 time
integration. The supporting numerical surface includes first-order and
Richtmyer/Lax--Wendroff finite-volume paths, modal DG degrees $p=0,1,2$ for
benchmark and sensitivity context, and generated stationary-Stokes
finite-element solves for initialization, refinement, and projection
diagnostics. The verification record combines manufactured-solution rows,
zero-forcing rest-state drift diagnostics, CFL, characteristic-speed, mass, and
positivity metadata, sampled self-convergence, native/SciML cross-integrator
checks, stationary-Stokes refinement, and resolved-velocity output-operator
checks; it also exposes that the present Rusanov/MUSCL flux--source realization
is not well balanced for the geometry-rest state. At $t=1.0$ s, section-averaged
axial velocities are compared with two resolved three-dimensional velocity
datasets using plane--tetrahedron quadrature; for C23 and C40, respectively,
the sectionwise area-mean velocity discrepancies have MAEs of 0.505 and
0.966 cm/s, RMSEs of 0.664 and 1.87 cm/s, and maximum absolute values of
2.35 and 9.92 cm/s. Because the wall models, boundary conditions, and transient
histories are not fully matched, these comparisons characterize differences
between computational outputs; they do not constitute a fully matched
one-dimensional--three-dimensional validation, physiological validation, or
support for pressure-ratio, FFR, or clinical claims.
