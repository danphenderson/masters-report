\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Abstract.}\par\smallskip
This report audits a reduced-order stenosis solver and a diagnostic comparison
with available resolved 3D velocity data. The implemented solver is the Julia
\path{StenosisHemodynamics} realization of an $R_{\max}$-normalized
Canic-derived one-dimensional area--flow model. Physical circular area and flow
are represented in solver coordinates by $a=R^2$ and
$q=Q_{\mathrm{phys}}/\pi$, so $A_{\mathrm{phys}}=\pi a$,
$Q_{\mathrm{phys}}=\pi q$, and the mean velocity is $q/a$. The principal
comparison realization uses the declared parabolic-profile baseline, Newtonian
rheology, a fixed-area characteristic boundary approximation, MUSCL finite
volume, minmod-limited states, Rusanov flux, source splitting, and native
SSPRK3.

The verification record gives positive but bounded manufactured-solution
evidence for the declared forced operator. It also exposes the central numerical
limitation: the current MUSCL/Rusanov realization does not preserve the
geometry-rest equilibrium. In zero-forcing C23/C40 rest runs, the artificial
flow retained at $t=1$ s is the same order as the production comparison-flow
scale. The comparison with resolved data is therefore interpreted only through a
declared plane--tetrahedron quadrature operator that computes section area,
physical flow, and area-mean axial velocity. Under that operator, the reported
C23/C40 section and radial-profile results are descriptive velocity
discrepancies for the available cases. Their interpretation is limited by
unmatched or unpersisted 3D wall, boundary, material, history, and
current/deformed geometry information, by unresolved axial variation in
extracted 3D flow, and by the $1.0$ s versus $0.9995$ s sample-time offset.
