\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Abstract.}\par\smallskip
This report develops a mathematical and numerical account of idealized stenotic
blood-flow simulation. The framework fixes the continuum, constitutive,
boundary-data, side-condition, model-hierarchy, and output conventions needed to
relate three-dimensional (3D) incompressible Navier--Stokes, fluid--structure
interaction, and generalized-Newtonian models to reduced 0D, 1D, 2D, and
coupled hemodynamic formulations. The numerical record has two bounded parts: a
$T=1$ resolved-velocity output-operator diagnostic for a selected 1D
area--flow realization, and a package benchmark suite for the Julia solver and
Python CLI surfaces. These computations support internal diagnostic and
reproducibility claims only; they do not establish clinical pressure evidence,
pressure-ratio accuracy, or resolved-3D validation.
