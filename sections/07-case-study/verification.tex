\subsection{Numerical Verification and Audit}
\label{sec:numerical-verification}

This section orders the numerical evidence for the implemented solver used in
the worked example. The evidence is read through the review categories above:
manufactured-solution verification, equilibrium preservation, boundary and CFL
diagnostics, and secondary implementation-health records.

\subsubsection{Evidence Hierarchy and Scope}
\label{subsec:verification-scope-convergence-stability}

The hierarchy used below is explicit. The strongest positive evidence is the
manufactured-solution record, because it compares the implemented forced
operator with a declared smooth exact solution and an independently assembled
forcing audit. The next and principal numerical limitation is the geometry-rest
preservation test, because the selected comparison runs start from the same rest
state and the current MUSCL/Rusanov realization does not exactly preserve it.
Boundary, CFL, positivity, and conservation diagnostics then state whether
accepted runs remained within the recorded numerical regime.
Self-convergence, backend parity, stationary-Stokes projection, broad
rheology/profile sweeps, resolved-velocity benchmark stages, and nonselected DG
rows are secondary implementation-health records and are kept in
Appendix~\ref{app:num-secondary-implementation-health}.

The observation operator has its own check because the retained 1D--3D
comparison is only meaningful after the resolved field is mapped to section
area, physical flow, and mean axial velocity. The synthetic record in
Table~\ref{tab:t1-cross-section-operator-validation} verifies the declared
plane--tetrahedron quadrature implementation on a single tetrahedron with
constant and affine node-centered axial fields. It checks that constant fields
return $\bar u=c$ and $Q=cA$, that affine triangle means integrate to the
centroid value up to roundoff, and that shifted planes produce the expected
area and mean-velocity changes. This is output-operator verification, not a
matched-data validation of the available 3D cases.

Standard finite-volume context is supplied by conservative hyperbolic
discretization with consistent numerical fluxes and sources, CFL-controlled time
stepping, limiter and Rusanov dissipation choices, and SSP Runge--Kutta time
integration \cite{LeVeque2002FiniteVolumeMethodsForHyperbolicProblems}. In 1D
blood-flow practice, MUSCL/Rusanov and Lax--Friedrichs-type discretizations are
commonly assessed through exact-solution, benchmark, grid-refinement, and
equilibrium tests
\cite{Wang2014BloodFlowNetworks,BeckersKolbe2025LaxFriedrichsHemodynamics,BoileauEtAl2015Benchmark,BenemeritoEtAl2024OpenBF,LuccaEtAl2025FFRPrediction}.
Here those records are read as empirical implementation evidence, not as a
formal convergence theorem for the full nonlinear stenosis solver.

\subsubsection{Manufactured-Solution Evidence}
\label{subsec:mms-verification}

The implementation exposes an opt-in manufactured forcing term. The production
equations use \texttt{NoForcing()} by default. For verification only,
\texttt{ManufacturedForcing()} supplies a smooth exact state
$(a^\star(z,t),q^\star(z,t))$, exact initial data, exact boundary states, and
source terms inserted into the existing finite-volume right-hand side. In solver
coordinates, let
\begin{flalign*}
    \quad
    s(z)&=\sin\!\left(\frac{\pi z}{L}\right),&
    a_0(z)&=R_0(z)^2,&
    \omega&=\frac{2\pi}{T_{\mathrm{mms}}}. &&
\end{flalign*}
The default manufactured record uses
\begin{flalign*}
    \quad
    \epsilon_a&=0.02,&
    U_{\mathrm{mms}}&=2.0\,\mathrm{cm}/\mathrm{s},&
    T_{\mathrm{mms}}&=0.02\,\mathrm{s}, &&
\end{flalign*}
and defines
\begin{flalign*}
    \quad
    a^\star(z,t)
    &=
    a_0(z)\left[1+\epsilon_a s(z)\cos(\omega t)\right], &&\\
    \quad
    q^\star(z,t)
    &=
    a_0(z)U_{\mathrm{mms}}s(z)^2\sin(\omega t). &&
\end{flalign*}
The tabulated run uses $0\le z\le L=6\,\mathrm{cm}$ and
$0\le t\le2\times10^{-3}\,\mathrm{s}$, equal to 10\% of the manufactured
period. Initial data are sampled from $(a^\star,q^\star)$ at $t=0$, and the
boundary-state routine receives the exact traces at $z=0$ and $z=L$. The
inserted forcing terms are the residuals required by the recorded flux and
source realization,
\begin{flalign*}
    \quad
    f_a(z,t)
    &=
    \pd_t a^\star+\pd_z F_a(a^\star,q^\star;z), &&\\
    \quad
    f_q(z,t)
    &=
    \pd_t q^\star+
    \pd_z F_q(a^\star,q^\star;z)-S_q(a^\star,q^\star;z), &&
\end{flalign*}
where $F_a,F_q$ and $S_q$ are the selected discrete-model point flux and source
functions. A separate residual-audit helper assembles the manufactured balance
outside the production right-hand-side call path and reports the maximum
absolute forcing mismatch in the generated table. The Julia test suite also
includes a mutation gate that deliberately perturbs the manufactured momentum
forcing and verifies that the MMS audit fails. The run therefore checks the
implemented operator against a declared manufactured record with an independent
forcing consistency audit; it remains an implementation-verification record, not
a physical stenosis experiment.

\begin{lstlisting}[basicstyle=\ttfamily\footnotesize,breaklines=true]
./scripts/stenosis-hemodynamics verify mms \
  --nxs 50,100,200,400 \
  --dt-values 2e-5,1e-5,5e-6,2.5e-6 \
  --tfinal 0.002 \
  --output-dir figures/static/static/tables/verification \
  --overwrite
\end{lstlisting}

The runner records cell-center discrete errors
\begin{flalign*}
    \quad
    \|e\|_1&=\frac{1}{N}\sum_i |e_i|,&
    \|e\|_2&=\left(\frac{1}{N}\sum_i e_i^2\right)^{1/2},&
    \|e\|_\infty&=\max_i |e_i|, &&
\end{flalign*}
with observed orders computed from the $L^2$ rows as
\begin{flalign*}
    \quad
    p_{\mathrm{obs}}
    &=
    \frac{\log(E_{\mathrm{coarse}}/E_{\mathrm{fine}})}
         {\log(\Delta_{\mathrm{coarse}}/\Delta_{\mathrm{fine}})}. &&
\end{flalign*}
For spatial rows $\Delta$ is the grid spacing. For requested-timestep rows the
native solver also records realized CFL diagnostics because the accepted
timestep is $\min(\Delta t_{\mathrm{request}},\Delta t_{\mathrm{CFL}})$. The
timestep-insensitivity rows in the generated MMS table are therefore read
through accepted timestep and realized CFL, not as a clean temporal-order study.

The spatial rows provide bounded, positive implementation-verification evidence

integrated error behavior, while $L^\infty$ highlights localized boundary,
limiter, or source-discretization effects.

\subsubsection{Geometry-Rest Preservation Test}
\label{subsec:rest-state-drift}

The continuous selected source-balanced law admits the geometry-rest state
$a_i=R_0(z_i)^2$, $q_i=0$ under compatible zero-flow boundary data
(Proposition~\ref{prop:selected-rest-state-compatibility}). The current
principal implementation is not exactly well balanced for that state. The
Rusanov/MUSCL operator includes numerical viscosity in the mass flux, and for
spatially varying $R_0$ this produces a nonzero discrete residual unless an
equilibrium reconstruction or path-conservative flux is added.

The zero-forcing rest runner starts the selected C23 and C40 geometries from the
rest state with a zero-velocity inlet and reports
\begin{flalign*}
    \quad
    &\max_i |q_i|,&
    &\max_i |a_i-R_0(z_i)^2|,&
    &\left|\sum_i a_i\Delta z-\sum_i a_i(0)\Delta z\right| &&
\end{flalign*}
as functions of grid size and elapsed time. This test is required context for
any comparison run initialized from the same geometry-rest state.

\begin{lstlisting}[basicstyle=\ttfamily\footnotesize,breaklines=true]
./scripts/stenosis-hemodynamics verify rest \
  --severities 23,40 \
  --nxs 100,200,400,800 \
  --elapsed-times 0,0.001,0.01,0.1,1.0 \
  --output-dir figures/static/static/tables/verification \
  --overwrite
\end{lstlisting}

The corrected rest-state run records zero requested and applied inlet flow, so
the previously reported $q_{\mathrm{in}}t$ volume signature is not retained.
The finite-volume balance
$\Delta\!\int a\,dz+\int(\widehat F^a_{\mathrm{out}}-\widehat F^a_{\mathrm{in}})\,dt$
closes to near roundoff in the generated CSV. The production comparison-flow
scale remains
$q_{\mathrm{comp}}\approx2.288/\pi=0.7283\,\mathrm{cm}^3/\mathrm{s}$ in the
solver coordinate, corresponding to
$Q_{\mathrm{comp}}\approx2.288\,\mathrm{cm}^3/\mathrm{s}$ in physical circular
flow, as used in the axial-flow comparison
(Figure~\ref{fig:t1-axial-flow-comparison}). On the $N=400$ production
comparison grid, the final zero-inlet rest-flow values are $0.03839$ and
$0.06339\,\mathrm{cm}^3/\mathrm{s}$ in solver $q$ for C23 and C40, equal to
5.3\% and 8.7\% of $q_{\mathrm{comp}}$. The corresponding peak production-grid
values are $0.04387$ and $0.07053\,\mathrm{cm}^3/\mathrm{s}$. On the finer
$N=800$ audit grid, the $t=1$ s values fall to $0.00973$ and
$0.01605\,\mathrm{cm}^3/\mathrm{s}$; the observed final-flow refinement rates
from $N=400$ to $N=800$ are 1.98 for both severities. The defect is therefore
strongly localized and strongly grid-decreasing, but it remains non-negligible
on the production comparison grid.

This geometry-rest result is the principal negative numerical finding of the
chapter. The selected C23/C40 comparison runs are initialized from the same
geometry-rest state and use the same operator. The corrected zero-inlet
magnitude no longer supports a large through-flow interpretation, but the
nonzero localized residual still prevents treating the comparison as a clean
discretization-accuracy study.

\subsubsection{Boundary, CFL, Positivity, and Conservation Diagnostics}
\label{subsec:boundary-diagnostics}

The native solver records accepted timestep extrema, realized CFL extrema,
$\lambda_-$ and $\lambda_+$ extrema, the minimum subcriticality margin
$\min(-\lambda_-,\lambda_+)$, solver-coordinate volume extrema and signed
defect, positivity projection count, and total area correction from the
positivity floor. The fixed-area characteristic boundary rule is used under the
subcritical sign condition $\lambda_-<0<\lambda_+$. A positive characteristic
radicand alone is not the boundary-regime evidence; the persisted sign extrema
and subcriticality margin are the relevant diagnostics.

In the rest-state table, the finest-grid C23/C40 $t=1$ s rows retain
positive subcritical margins, zero requested and applied inlet flow, signed
solver-volume defects, boundary-flux integrals, and near-roundoff finite-volume
balance residuals. The generated rest CSV records zero positivity projection
counts for these rest tests. The production C23/C40 comparison diagnostics in
Table~\ref{tab:t1-alpha-radicand-diagnostics} provide the corresponding
characteristic-speed evidence for the runs later used in the velocity-output
comparison. These diagnostics are admissibility and bookkeeping checks. They do
not remove the non-well-balanced geometry-rest drift reported above.

\subsubsection{Secondary Implementation-Health Checks}
\label{subsec:verification-summary}

The fourth evidence tier is kept out of the main argument. Appendix~\ref{app:num-secondary-implementation-health}
retains the package benchmark stage summary, sampled self-convergence figure,
backend-parity figure, nonselected DG p/h demonstration, resolved-velocity
benchmark stage, and broad rheology/profile sensitivity figure. Appendix~\ref{app:num-stationary-stokes-refinement}
retains the fixed-wall stationary-Stokes projection and mesh-refinement record.
Those records are useful implementation-health checks: they can reveal runnable
surface regressions, boundedness problems, backend-policy discrepancies, or
sensitivity to nonprincipal descriptors. They do not replace the MMS record, and
they do not change the geometry-rest conclusion.
