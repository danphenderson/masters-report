\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Coronary and arterial stenoses motivate a basic computational question: how do
geometry, wall properties, rheology, and boundary data determine the state of
blood flow through a narrowed vessel? Clinical pressure-ratio measurements show
why anatomical narrowing alone does not determine functional obstruction, but
this report stays on the mathematical and numerical side of that problem. Its
goal is to review the hierarchy of the single-vessel hemodynamic models and to
present bounded numerical diagnostics for an idealized ($C^\infty$) model of
stenosed vessels.

The report fixes the vocabulary needed to move from continuum assumptions, to
the three-dimensional incompressible Navier--Stokes and generalized-Newtonian
settings, to reduced 0D/1D/2D hemodynamic model classes, and finally to a
selected one-dimensional numerical record for an idealized stenosis. Its
contribution is to organize the relevant mathematical and numerical literature,
frame a controlled resolved-velocity diagnostic for a smooth idealized stenosed
vessel, and record package-benchmark evidence for the implemented solver and
CLI surfaces.

Pressure-ratio quantities in this report are computed model outputs, not
clinical FFR or CT-FFR measurements, unless an external measurement comparison
is explicitly introduced. Convention~\ref{conv:appendix-pressure-reference-ratio}
in Appendix~\ref{app:domain-specific-notation} records the pressure-reference,
gauge-pressure, high-flow-window, and admissibility conventions used for any
reduced pressure-ratio output. The numerical record in
Section~\ref{sec:resolved-3d-comparison} reports a velocity diagnostic and
package benchmark suite only; it does not yet report pressure-drop or
pressure-ratio outputs. Those conventions are included to fix the output
vocabulary for subsequent reduced-order studies.



\subsection*{Literature Review Roadmap}

\addcontentsline{toc}{subsection}{Literature Review Roadmap}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Section~\ref{subsec:continuum-constitutive-assumptions} fixes the continuum,
kinematic, balance-law, and constitutive assumptions used throughout the report.
    \item Section~\ref{subsec:governing-equations-model-hierarchy} states the governing
incompressible Navier--Stokes and generalized-Newtonian setting and then records
the selected one-dimensional area--flow reduction.
    \item Section~\ref{subsec:model-hierarchy-comparison-boundary} places 0D, 1D, 2D,
3D, and multiscale models into a comparison hierarchy and clarifies what each
tier can and cannot support. Section~\ref{subsec:pressure-flow-motivation}
motivates pressure-drop, pressure-ratio, and shear-output conventions from the
clinical anatomy--function distinction.
    \item Section~\ref{sec:resolved-3d-comparison} reports the preliminary
resolved-velocity diagnostic at $T=1$ and the package benchmark suite.
Section~\ref{sec:conclusions-limitations}
summarizes the limitations and identifies the next evidence needed for stronger
pressure--flow claims.
\end{itemize}
