\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Coronary and arterial stenoses motivate a basic computational question: how do
geometry, wall properties, rheology, and boundary data affect blood flow through
a narrowed vessel? Anatomical narrowing alone does not determine functional
obstruction, so a useful computational report must distinguish the physical
quantity being predicted, the model tier that produces it, and the evidence
used to check the computation. This report stays on the mathematical and
numerical side of that problem. It studies a $C^\infty$ idealized stenosis
geometry, an extended one-dimensional area--flow realization, and a bounded
comparison with resolved 3D velocity data.

\subsection{Research Problem and Questions}
\label{subsec:research-problem-questions}

The research problem is to make the implemented stenosis solver auditable as a
mathematical and numerical object. The report therefore asks three questions:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item What continuous model, solver-coordinate map, closure choices,
    boundary approximation, and discrete operator define the implemented 1D
    stenosis solver?
    \item Does the implementation converge on a smooth manufactured solution
    and preserve the geometry-rest equilibrium?
    \item Under the declared plane-quadrature operator, what velocity
    discrepancies are measured against the available resolved 3D data, and
    which unmatched conditions limit interpretation?
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Objectives and Contributions}
\label{subsec:objectives-contributions}

The contribution is deliberately narrow:
\begin{itemize}
    \item an authoritative implemented-model specification;
    \item bounded MMS-based verification evidence;
    \item exposure of a material rest-equilibrium failure;
    \item a declared 1D--3D velocity observation operator;
    \item descriptive C23/C40 discrepancy localization.
\end{itemize}
Together, these items define a numerical audit and diagnostic cross-model
velocity comparison. The report makes no broader validation, pressure-accuracy,
FFR, physiological, clinical, predictive, or causal claim.

\subsection{Scope and Limits of the Study}
\label{subsec:scope-limits}

These closing subsections define the report's active claim boundary for the
completed work. Section~\ref{sec:methodology} is the implementation contract:
it records the $R_{\max}$-normalized Canic-derived area--flow model, the
solver-coordinate map, the closure choices, the fixed-area characteristic
boundary approximation, and the active discrete operator. The principal
comparison realization is identified only at the level needed for the audit:
parabolic-profile baseline, Newtonian rheology, MUSCL finite volume with minmod
limiting, Rusanov flux, source splitting, and native SSPRK3.

Section~\ref{sec:numerical-verification} treats MMS as bounded
implementation-verification evidence and treats the geometry-rest result as a
material failure of the current Rusanov/MUSCL realization. At $t=1$ s, the
artificial flow retained by this realization is the same order as the production
comparison-flow scale, so the failure is a principal numerical limitation
rather than a secondary caveat. Section~\ref{sec:resolved-3d-comparison}
uses the declared plane--tetrahedron quadrature operator to compare C23/C40
section velocities and reconstructed radial profiles. That comparison stops at
descriptive discrepancy localization because the available 3D wall, boundary,
material, history, and current/deformed geometry information is unmatched or not
persisted; axial 3D flow variation and sample-time alignment remain evidential
gates.

\subsection{Report Organization}
\label{subsec:report-organization}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Section~\ref{sec:background-literature-review} reviews only the
    background needed for stenosis-aware 1D closures, well-balanced behavior,
    manufactured-solution verification, and declared cross-dimensional velocity
    observables.
    \item Section~\ref{sec:methodology} specifies the model variables,
    solver-coordinate convention, closures, boundary approximation,
    finite-volume/time-integration realization, comparison operator, metrics,
    and provenance record.
    \item Section~\ref{sec:numerical-verification} reports the positive MMS
    evidence and the non-well-balanced geometry-rest drift that constrains the
    later comparison.
    \item Section~\ref{sec:resolved-3d-comparison} reports the C23/C40 velocity
    discrepancies under the declared observation operator and localizes the
    largest section and radial-profile differences.
    \item Sections~\ref{sec:discussion} and
    \ref{sec:conclusions-limitations} answer the three research questions,
    interpret the evidence gates, and close with the bounded contribution.
\end{itemize}
